# OSINT Reconnaissance

## Overview

This section focuses on Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) reconnaissance techniques used to gather publicly accessible information about systems, domains, infrastructure, and exposed services.

The labs in this section demonstrate passive reconnaissance methodologies commonly used during the early stages of cybersecurity assessments, penetration testing, and threat intelligence operations.

Reconnaissance activities performed in this section include:
- exposed service discovery
- subdomain enumeration
- email harvesting
- public infrastructure analysis
- attack surface identification
- passive intelligence gathering

---

# Objectives

The primary objectives of this section were:

- Understand passive reconnaissance workflows
- Identify publicly accessible infrastructure
- Discover exposed services and metadata
- Enumerate subdomains
- Harvest publicly available email addresses
- Analyze attack surface exposure
- Practice OSINT methodologies within authorized environments

# Lessons Learned

- Publicly accessible information can reveal valuable infrastructure details.
- Passive reconnaissance reduces detection risk during information gathering.
- Exposed services and metadata increase attack surface visibility.
- OSINT techniques support vulnerability analysis and security assessments.
- Public DNS records and search indexing may unintentionally expose sensitive information.
